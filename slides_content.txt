I. The Problem: The Hidden Cost of "Business as Usual" (0:00 - 0:45)

(Slide: Global Emissions / The Grid's Dark Side)

"Every time you flip a light switch in a typical home, 
you’re connected to a system that is—quite literally—heating the planet. 
Right now, the average US household is responsible for nearly 9,000 pounds or 4.7 metric tonnes
of CO2 emissions every single year just from electricity.

Conventional power generation is over 12 times more carbon-intensive than solar. 
While coal and natural gas pump up to 1,000 grams of CO2 into our atmosphere for 
every single kilowatt-hour, a rooftop solar panel produces that same energy for just 41 grams. 
The difference isn't just a metric; it's the difference between a sustainable future and an 
environmental collapse. But homeowners are stuck. They want to help, but they lack the tools to see 
the direct, measurable impact their own roof can make. That’s why we built Solwards."



II. The Solution: From Data to Action (0:45 - 1:10)

(Slide: Solwards - Mapping the Green Transition)
"Solwards is more than a calculator; it’s a transparency engine. 
We bridge the gap between high-level NASA climate data and your specific front door. 
By integrating Google Maps with real-time irradiance metrics and EIA cost data, 
we turn an abstract environmental problem into a tangible, personal solution.

We provide every user with a comprehensive Solar Score, 
but we don't stop there. Our backend performs a deep-dive financial audit—
calculating everything from 25-year ROI and Net Profit to the exact Payback Period in years. 
We don't just tell you solar is 'good'; we show you exactly how many tons of carbon you’ll keep 
out of the atmosphere and exactly how many dollars you'll keep in your pocket."


III. The Demo (1:00 - 2:15)
(Live Demo or Video Walkthrough)
"Let’s look at how it works.

The Entry: Our homepage is designed to inspire. We want users to feel the impact they could be making. One click takes us to the Map Interface.

Precision Mapping: Here, we’ve integrated a search bar for any address. But here’s the magic: instead of a circular radius, we use a custom polygon tool. As I click and drag these points, I’m defining the exact surface area of my available roof space.

The Analysis: Once the polygon is closed, we hit 'Analyze.' At this moment, our backend API is doing the heavy lifting. It takes those latitude and longitude coordinates, cross-references them with regional solar irradiance data, and calculates the fiscal outlook based on local energy rates.

The Result: The modal you see now is the 'Solar Score.' It’s a comprehensive breakdown: energy generated per day, estimated cost savings, and—crucially—the environmental impact in CO2 reduction. We even provide an equity analysis to show how this investment scales over time."


IV. The Impact & Closing (2:20 - 2:45)

(Slide: The Future of Solwards / Thank You)

"If we increased residential solar by just 15%, we could slash 8.5 million metric tons of CO2 annually. 
That is the goal of Solwards: to move homeowners from 'thinking about it' to 'acting on it.' 
By making the invisible costs of conventional energy visible, we’re empowering everyone to take their 
first step toward a carbon-neutral home.
